About the Program

A focused week inside
a real university lab.

This version of the workshop is built around one single week, one small cohort, and one immersive experience centered on cells, light, imaging, and scientific observation.

01

Official Program Theme

Students join a workshop titled Seeing the Invisible: Exploring Cells with Light, designed to introduce them to the world of live-cell imaging and the hidden dynamics inside living systems.

Live-cell imaging Microscopy Cell biology
02

One Dedicated Week

This website is for the July 20 to July 24, 2026 workshop only. Students attend a single five-day program with the same team, same schedule, and same progression throughout the week.

July 20 to 24 Monday to Friday 10 AM to 3 PM
03

Balanced Structure

Each day combines conceptual learning with practical work. Students begin with scientific foundations, share lunch together, and then move into hands-on culture and imaging work in the afternoon.

Lecture Hands-on lab Small-group format
Daily Schedule

Hour by hour.

The week follows a consistent daily rhythm so students can build confidence, ask deeper questions, and gradually work more directly with real scientific tools and methods.

1
10 to 12

Physics Lecture with Prof. Comert Kural

A structured morning session focused on core scientific ideas, physical principles, and the conceptual foundations needed to understand light, imaging, and living systems.

2
12 to 1

Lunch

Students eat together, reset for the afternoon, and continue informal conversations with instructors and peers in a relaxed setting.

3
1 to 3

Hands-On Class with Dr. Omer Kural

Afternoon instruction centered on culturing and imaging cancer cells, with direct exposure to laboratory thinking, practical workflows, and visual observation.

4
Mon to Thu

Progressive Skill Building

Students revisit techniques across the week, moving from guided explanation to more confident interaction with real procedures and scientific images.

5
Friday

Final Reflection and Closing

The last day brings the week together through discussion, synthesis, and reflection on what students observed, learned, and discovered in the lab environment.

Why this workshop?

This program is designed for students who want something concrete, serious, and inspiring. It offers a direct look at how scientific questions are approached in a real lab.

Rather than overwhelming students with a huge program, this workshop keeps the schedule focused, approachable, and deeply connected to one compelling scientific theme.
