About the Program

Built for curious students.
Designed around real research.

No advanced background required, just motivation, curiosity, and a willingness to engage seriously with science. This program meets students where they are and brings them further than they expect.

01

Who Should Apply

Any high school student with a genuine interest in physics, biophysics, imaging, quantitative biology, computation, or scientific problem-solving. Beginners are welcome.

Weekly Structure

Five days. Five chapters.

Each session follows a carefully sequenced five-day arc, moving from orientation to hands-on work to independent exploration and final presentation.

1
MON

Orientation & Scientific Foundations

Welcome, lab safety, overview of the research theme, and the first guided activity.

2
TUE

Instrumentation & Data Collection

Direct work with scientific equipment, imaging tools, and quantitative measurement workflows.

3
WED

Analysis & Interpretation

Analyzing observations and learning how scientists move from raw evidence to careful conclusions.

4
THU

Research Extension & Mentorship

Deepening project work while meeting mentors from different levels of science training.

5
FRI

Presentation & Reflection

Students present their findings and reflect on how scientific investigation develops over time.

Why This Program?

Most science education gives students answers. This workshop teaches them how to ask better questions and how to pursue them with rigor, creativity, and purpose.

Designed by Prof. Comert Kural and delivered by a dedicated team, the workshop creates a genuine bridge between high school science and university-level research.
