Modern research experience for high school students

Hands-on summer science in an Ohio State inspired setting.

The Kural Research Workshop is designed to introduce high school students to authentic scientific thinking through guided laboratory experiences, quantitative analysis, scientific discussion, and mentorship from researchers across multiple training levels.

The program will offer two separate five-day sessions in July 2026. Each session runs Monday through Friday and follows the same core experience, allowing students to choose the week that best matches their summer schedule.

About the program

This workshop is built for students who are curious about science and want meaningful exposure to research environments, scientific tools, and the process of asking good questions. No advanced background is required. Motivation, curiosity, and a willingness to engage seriously matter most.

Who should apply

High school students with interest in physics, biophysics, quantitative biology, imaging, computation, or scientific problem solving.

What students will experience

The workshop will combine hands-on activities, close instruction, and scientific discussion. Specific modules can be customized later, but the structure below gives the site a polished and professional framework now.

Sample weekly flow

Day 1
Orientation and scientific foundations

Welcome, introductions, safety expectations, overview of the scientific theme, and the first guided research activity.

Day 2
Instrumentation and data collection

Students begin working directly with scientific equipment, imaging tools, quantitative measurements, or computational workflows.

Day 3
Analysis and interpretation

Participants analyze observations, discuss patterns, and learn how scientists move from raw evidence to careful conclusions.

Day 4
Research extension and mentorship

Students continue project work while meeting instructors and mentors from different levels of science training.

Day 5
Presentation and reflection

Students present what they explored, what they learned, and how scientific investigation develops over time.

Program highlights

  • Exposure to real scientific methods rather than only textbook concepts
  • Instruction from faculty, researchers, and workshop staff
  • Emphasis on curiosity, discipline, observation, and thoughtful analysis
  • Supportive environment for students with different academic backgrounds
  • Professional academic atmosphere with strong mentorship

Who teaches in the workshop

The workshop model is built around multiple levels of scientific training. This allows students to learn from faculty leadership while also connecting with instructors and mentors who can make research feel approachable, concrete, and inspiring.

  • Faculty
  • Researchers and instructors
  • Coordinators and volunteers
  • Future additions such as graduate or undergraduate mentors

Professional atmosphere

The goal is to provide a workshop environment that feels serious, welcoming, organized, and intellectually energizing. Students should leave with greater confidence, stronger curiosity, and a more realistic picture of scientific work.
